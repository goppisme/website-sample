Hyderabadi Biryani recipe

Hyderabadi Biryani, also called dum biryani, is a fragrant rice dish from Hyderabad, India.It features layers of basmati rice and marinated meat (usually goat or chicken) cooked with aromatic spices.The slow cooking process, known as "dum," infuses the rice with rich flavors and creates a tender meat.Saffron, kewra water, and rose water add a unique touch to this flavorful biryani.There are two main types: "kacchi" where raw meat is cooked with rice, and "pakki" where cooked meat is layered with rice.

Ingredients

  1. For the Chicken Marinade:
    • 1/2 kg chicken (bone-in pieces, preferably thighs and drumsticks)
    • 2/3 cup yogurt
    • 1-1.5 tsp red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
    • 1-1.5 tsp biryani masala
    • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
    • 2 slit green chilies
    • 1.25 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
    • Salt to taste
  2. For the Rice:
    • 1.5 cups basmati rice (aged, rinsed, and soaked for 30 minutes)
    • 4 tbsp vegetable oil (or ghee)
    • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
    • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves (cilantro)
    • 2 tbsp chopped mint leaves
    • 2 tsp saffron strands (soaked in 3 tbsp hot milk)
    • Salt to taste

Process

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, combine yogurt, red chili powder, biryani masala, turmeric powder, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, cardamom powder, and salt. Add chicken pieces and mix well to coat them evenly. Cover and marinate for at least 30 minutes, preferably overnight for deeper flavors.
  2. Cook the Rice: In a large pot, bring enough water (around 4 cups) to a boil. Add the soaked basmati rice, salt, and a few drops of oil. Cook according to package instructions or until the rice is 80% cooked but still has a slight bite. Drain the rice and spread it on a plate to cool slightly.
  3. Assemble the Biryani: Heat oil (or ghee) in a heavy-bottomed pot or pressure cooker. Fry the sliced onions until golden brown. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook over medium heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Layering: In the pot with cooked chicken and onions, spread half of the cooked rice. Sprinkle with half of the chopped coriander leaves, mint leaves, and a few drops of saffron milk. Repeat the layer with remaining rice, herbs, and saffron milk.
  5. Cooking (Pot Method): Cover the pot tightly with a lid and seal with foil if using a regular pot. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and flavors are absorbed.
  6. Cooking (Pressure Cooker Method): Close the pressure cooker lid and cook for 3-4 whistles on medium heat. Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes before opening the lid.
  7. Garnish and Serve: Once cooked, gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Garnish with additional chopped coriander leaves and fried onions (optional). Serve hot with raita or your favorite chutney.

Tips

  • Using aged basmati rice helps prevent mushiness.
  • Adjust the amount of red chili powder according to your spice preference.
  • You can substitute chicken with mutton (goat meat) for a richer flavor.
    • For a more intense aroma, add a few cloves and bay leaves while frying the onions.
